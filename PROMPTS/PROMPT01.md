1. Sprint Description: Procedural Endgame Anomalies (Priority: Highest) Context: Once the static Grimoire is fully unlocked, the puzzle loop risks becoming repetitive as the solution space is perfectly mapped. Task: Implement a procedural ghost generation engine for late-game "Schleierbrüche" (Veil Tears). Dynamically combine traits, tells, and factions from DATA.GHOSTS and DATA.TRAITS to create entirely new, undocumented entities that force players to rely purely on deductive reasoning rather than memorization.

2. Sprint Description: Experimental Artifact Crafting (Priority: High) Context: Essences are currently only used to generate ÄK via sales or to fuel Grimoire studies. Task: Expand the functionality of the Labor (Lab). Introduce a crafting matrix where surplus essences can be forged into single-use, experimental wards or permanent equipment modifications. This provides a crucial resource sink for the late-game economy.

3. Sprint Description: Hunter Quirks and Legacy System (Priority: High) Context: Hunters currently level up and gain skills, but lack distinct personalities or long-term attachment mechanics. Task: Assign procedural quirks to hunters upon recruitment (e.g., "Nyctophobia" or "Iron Affinity"). These traits should dynamically modify execution chances based on the active environmental tags of a location. Additionally, implement a legacy system where retiring a max-level hunter passes a unique, permanent perk to the candidate pool.

4. Sprint Description: HQ Invasions & Base Defense (Priority: High) Context: The HQ remains a completely safe haven regardless of the global veil pressure or the state of its home district. Task: Trigger defensive puzzle encounters within the Garage or Archiv when the local district veil reaches critical thresholds (e.g., >90). If the player fails to banish the invader, a random HQ room is temporarily downgraded, adding tangible stakes to poor map management.

5. Sprint Description: Forward Operating Sensors & Automation (Priority: Medium) Context: Resolving low-tier routine contracts manually becomes tedious during the late-game grind. Task: Allow players to invest ÄK to install automated sensor grids in secured districts. These grids should act as autonomous software agents that interact directly with the simulation's data interfaces to resolve Tier 0 and Tier 1 contracts in the background. Because automated agents bypass UI button metaphors entirely, this system will integrate cleanly into the background Sim-Loop without requiring manual player input.

6. Sprint Description: Dynamic Narrative Event Hook (Priority: Medium) Context: The day-to-day progression is purely mechanical, driven by contract generation and wage deductions. Task: Implement a daily event hook in Engine.nextDay. Trigger localized text-events (e.g., "The Mayor demands a bribe" or "A hunter is cursed") that force players to make binary choices affecting ÄK, reputation, or hunter stress.

7. Sprint Description: Rival Hunter Agencies (Priority: Medium) Context: There is no external pressure on the contract board; contracts only expire based on their individual timers. Task: Introduce a simulated rival agency that actively consumes contracts from the map. This forces players to prioritize high-value or high-threat targets quickly before they are stolen, adding a competitive economic layer.

8. Sprint Description: Map-Altering Veil Effects (Priority: Medium-Low) Context: High veil pressure changes numbers (payouts, variant tiers), but not the tangible layout of the locations. Task: Allow locations to temporarily "mutate" their environmental tags when their district veil exceeds 80. For example, an Urban location could suddenly gain the "Sanctified" tag due to anomalous shifting, forcing players to dynamically adapt their standard loadouts.

9. Sprint Description: Multi-Stage Legendary Expeditions (Priority: Low) Context: Boss and Legend encounters, including the Schleierfürst, are currently resolved in a single location phase. Task: Create chained contract sequences. Diagnosing and surviving the first location merely reveals the true identity and subsequent location of the Legend, requiring sustained roster management and multiple deployments to fully clear the threat.

10. Sprint Description: Advanced Procedural Rendering Polish (Priority: Low) Context: The generative canvas rendering is highly functional, but can be visually enhanced to reflect the mutating environments. Task: Refine the generative rendering pipeline in gfx.js to visually reflect environmental decay and high veil pressure directly within the scene view. Ensure strict masking and sprite layering rules during these rendering passes so that mutating background terrain does not incorrectly blend into the isolated subject sprites of the ghosts or hunters.
